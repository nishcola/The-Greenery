Beyond the Bin: Our Composting Journey

Published on 2/26/2025 by The Greenery Team

At The Greenery, sustainability isn't just a buzzword – it's a part of us. One crucial aspect of this is minimizing waste, and a huge part of that effort involves composting. But what exactly happens to those veggie peels and coffee grounds after they leave our kitchen?

Why Compost? Reducing Landfill Load

When organic waste like food scraps ends up in a landfill, it decomposes anaerobically (without oxygen). This process releases methane, a potent greenhouse gas significantly more harmful than carbon dioxide. By composting, we divert tons of organic material from the landfill each year, drastically reducing our contribution to greenhouse gas emissions.

Our Process: Partnering for Impact

We meticulously separate all acceptable pre-consumer food scraps in our kitchen – things like vegetable trimmings, fruit peels, coffee grounds, and tea leaves. These scraps are collected regularly by our fantastic local partner, CompostNow. They operate a commercial composting facility where our scraps are combined with other organic materials like yard waste.

Through carefully managed processes involving aeration and temperature control, microorganisms break down these materials into nutrient-rich compost, essentially creating high-quality soil amendment.

Closing the Loop: From Scraps to Soil

This finished compost is then used by local farms (including some we source produce from!), community gardens, and landscapers, returning valuable nutrients to the soil. It improves soil structure, water retention, and reduces the need for synthetic fertilizers. It's a beautiful example of a circular system – turning what could have been waste into a valuable resource that helps grow more food.

Our Commitment Continues

While composting handles unavoidable scraps, our primary goal is always waste reduction. We continuously refine our menu planning, ordering, and preparation techniques (like using veggie scraps for stock) to minimize surplus in the first place. Our composting program is a vital backstop in our larger mission to operate as sustainably as possible.

We're proud of our composting efforts and our partnership with CompostNow. It's one more way we strive to nourish both our customers and our planet.
